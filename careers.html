The FRC Field Tech Advisors (FTA) are responsible for making sure that the FRC field is running smoothly, and are consulted whenever there is a technical error. I think it takes at least a few hours of training to become an FTA, not including the hours needed to initially become an FTAA (Field Tech Assistant Advisor). They need to know how to repair each element and diagnose possible problems that could happen. The FTAs need to learn a bit of new technology every year because of the changing game elements, although some characteristics may be similar to previous years. The FMS (field management system) used at all events is the same and doesn’t change much throughout the years, so FTAs only need to learn it initially.
