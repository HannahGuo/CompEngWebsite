Unit 1 - Hardware, Software & OS

Computer Assembly Process

To disassemble the computer, we first unscrewed the side plate to expose the rest of the parts of the computer in the case. We also lay the computer down on its side to make the screws more accessible. From there, we worked quickly to remove all the parts in the steps that were given. We removed the hard drive and the CD-ROM (optical disk drive) from the case, followed by removing the network card, the sound card, the RAM chips from their slots on the motherboard. Then, we took out the power supply. Finally, once all the cables were unplugged from the motherboard, we unscrewed it and took it out too. We used a Phillips screwdriver to unscrew the screws that held the optical disk drive and motherboard to the case.

I didn’t find taking the computer apart difficult at all, though reassembling took a bit longer because we had to plug all the wires back into their appropriate locations. We were also very careful in assembling parts in order not to damage them. I found the overall experience to be really interesting since this was the first time I’ve ever taken apart and reassembled a desktop. I always thought that building a computer was intimidating since there were so many parts, wires and specifications to keep track of. However, I was surprised at how easy it is to do once you have the knowledge needed.
